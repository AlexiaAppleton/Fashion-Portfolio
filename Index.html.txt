<section id="about" class="section">

<h2 class="section-title hidden">About</h2>

<div class="about hidden">

My fashion practice is driven by concept-led design and experimentation, with a strong focus on construction, pattern making, and development. I am most interested in how ideas can be communicated through shape, structure and material rather than relying solely on surface design. Designers such as Duran Lantink, Vivienne Westwood and Gabriela Hearst have influenced the way I approach my work, particularly in how they challenge fast fashion, traditional silhouettes and the relationship between fashion and sustainability.
A key project within my portfolio was a conceptual fashion brief exploring the suffocating nature of the fast fashion industry. I created a large-scale abstract piece that focused on restriction, excess and discomfort. Through this project, I worked with exaggerated proportions and engulfing forms to visually represent pressure and overwhelm. This was an important learning experience, pushing me to think beyond conventional garments and focus on translating an idea into a physical outcome.
Another project that reflects my creative process was a painting brief where I painted directly onto a jacket in which I designed and made myself. For this project, I took inspiration from the likes of Dan McKinleys experimental spray paint work and the silhouettes created by @bitcrcrcr’s work with White Abyss. This allowed me to explore the crossover between fashion and fine art while still considering the structure and wearability of the piece. Painting onto a constructed garment helped me develop my understanding of how colour, texture and imagery interact with form and movement.
I consider pattern making and garment development to be the most successful elements of my work. I enjoy the technical challenge of transforming conceptual ideas into structured designs and refining them through sampling and development. These skills have allowed me to communicate my ideas clearly and form a strong foundation for my future progression in fashion design.
</div>
