<h2 class="section-title hidden">About</h2>

<div class="about hidden">

My portfolio presents a diverse range of projects that explore observation, storytelling and material experimentation through fashion and design. Each project reflects my interest in translating conceptual narratives into expressive, wearable outcomes.
 
The first project draws inspiration from jesters, figures traditionally associated with comedy and playfulness, yet historically rooted in truth-telling and social critique. This duality became central to my design approach. I explored the contrast between a playful exterior and the seriousness beneath the jester’s identity through material and silhouette. Recycled denim scraps and chaotic ruffles symbolised disruption and imbalance, while structured corsetry represented restraint and control. Experimental sampling informed my use of texture and movement, allowing humour and satire to coexist with theatrical detail. Loose ribbons threaded through structured panels imply tension, while the deliberately messy, heavily ruffled skirt creates a chaotic silhouette that reflects the performative nature of fools. The final corset and skirt ensemble embodies contradiction and complexity, reinforcing themes of honesty, deception and theatrical entertainment.
 
The second project focused on observing people in their everyday lives and reinterpreting these moments in an expressive and visually impactful way. I captured ordinary activities in natural settings, paying close attention to hand gestures and body language. Using bright colours, loose lines and expressionist techniques, I highlighted energy and emotion within routine moments. Photography formed the foundation of this project and directly informed my designs. I collaged my drawings onto a waistcoat, transforming the garment into a wearable canvas. The choice of a waistcoat, associated with casual everyday wear, reinforced the concept both visually and conceptually.
The third project centres on nature as a source of texture, colour and pattern. I explored expressive sampling techniques and designed my own repeat pattern using a palette of mossy greens and bark browns, translating organic forms into a refined final outcome.


</div>
